# Glossary — Macedonian Phalanx (Taylor)

*Definitions grounded in Taylor's own Glossary (L4000) and chapter text. Sizes are the canonical "ideal" figures of Polybius and the tacticians; real units varied.*

## Units & organization (smallest → largest)
- **Lochos** — general word for a military unit; most often the **file** (men one behind another), the phalanx's basic building block.
- **Dekas / dekad** ("ten") — Alexander-era name for the **file**; originally perhaps literally 10 men, but by the end of Alexander's reign planned at 16, so the name had lost its literal sense.
- **Syntagma** — the smallest *independent* subunit, ideally **256 men = 16 files × 16 men** (a square, so it could hear commands from every side). Polybius calls it a *semeia*/*speira* and likens it to the Roman maniple.
- **Speira** — Polybius' word for the Roman **maniple**; used loosely for small units.
- **Taxis** — generic "body of soldiers"; also the **largest subdivision of Alexander's phalanx, ~1,500 men**, and separately a half-*syntagma* of 128 men in the tacticians.
- **Chiliarchy** (*chiliarchia*) — **1,000 (ideally 1,024) men = four syntagmata**; a Persian-derived level.

## Formation orders & spacing
- **Open order** — the natural 4-cubit (~2 m) interval per man, in length and depth; the "march order," used advancing on the enemy.
- **Pyknosis / close order** — the intermediate 2-cubit (~1 m) interval; the standard **battle order**, on which Polybius bases his sarissa-projection calculations.
- **Synaspismos** ("locked shields") — the most compact 1-cubit (~0.5 m) interval, shield-to-shield; Asclepiodotus treats it as a **static defensive** formation ("when the enemy is marching upon us"). Taylor: often used loosely just to mean "close order."

## Equipment
- **Sarissa** — the long two-handed Macedonian **pike**, the defining weapon; length varied but roughly **5–8 m** (Polybius: 16-cubit design, 14 in practice, ~10 cubits projecting).
- **Doru / dory** — the general Greek word for **spear**; the one-handed ~2 m spear of the Classical hoplite.
- **Aspis** — the large (usually round, ~1 m) heavy-infantry shield of the Classical hoplite.
- **Pelte / pelta** — a **smaller, rimless shield** carried by the phalangite (the two-handed sarissa grip forbade the big *aspis*); gives "peltast" its name.
- **Hypaspists** ("shield-bearers") — the **royal infantry guard** of Philip/Alexander; later **Silver Shields** (*Argyraspides*) / Antigonid **Peltasts**.
- **Peltast** — *pelte*-carrying infantry: light skirmishers in the 5th–4th c., **medium infantry** in the Hellenistic period; also the elite/younger phalanx element.

## The tacticians & historians (Taylor's key sources)
- **Asclepiodotus** — earliest surviving drill manual (~100 BC); primary source for intervals, unit sizes, equipment.
- **Aelian** — Roman-era tactician copying the same tradition; his *Tactics* became the blueprint for Early Modern European pike drill.
- **Arrian** — wrote a *Tactics* and an anti-Alan battle order; also the main narrative historian of Alexander.
- **Polybius** — the **only contemporary historian of the Hellenistic phalanx** (mid-2nd c. BC); source of the canonical sarissa figures and the legion-vs-phalanx analysis (18.28–32).
