# Macedonian Phalanx — Cheatsheet (Taylor 2020)

Quick-reference formation tables. For computation use `scripts/advise.py`; every number is in `data/model.json` with line citations. **1 cubit ≈ 1.5 ft ≈ 0.5 m.**

## Order & spacing (per man, both width and depth)
| Order | Cubits | Metres | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Open | 4 | ~2.0 | march / advance |
| **Close (pyknosis)** | **2** | **~1.0** | **standard battle order** |
| Synaspismos (locked shields) | 1 | ~0.5 | static defence / receive charge |

## Depth
| Context | Ranks |
|---|---|
| Standard Macedonian (Hellenistic) | **16** |
| Early Alexander / Issus, and hoplite | 8 |
| Doubled (e.g. Magnesia) | 32 |

Only the first **~5 ranks** (6 in Aelian/Arrian) reach the enemy. Deeper = push, morale, replacements — **not** more reach or frontage.

## The sarissa & its hedge
- Length **5–8 m** (10–16 cubits); typically ~6–7 m. Polybius: 16-cubit design → 14 in practice.
- Rank interval **2 cubits**. Grip loss: **4 cubits** (Polybius, counterweight) or 2 (rear-grip).
- 14-cubit sarissa → front man's points reach **10 cubits**; ranks project **10 / 8 / 6 / 4 / 2 cubits** → **5 ranks** of points ahead of each front-rank man.

## Frontage math (close order, 1 m/man)
`frontage_m = (men ÷ depth) × spacing_m`. A **16,000-man phalanx** = 1,000 files = **1,000 m front × 16 m deep**. A syntagma (256, 16 deep) = a **16 m square**.

## Density vs Rome
At close order a Macedonian takes **half** a Roman's frontage → each front-rank Roman faces **2 Macedonians + ~10 sarissa points** (Polybius 18.30).

## Designer's one-liner
Unbeatable as a **continuous frontal hedge on flat ground** (10 points per Roman), but it has no reserves, fights only forward, and any **gap, flank, rear, or broken ground** is fatal — so depth past ~5 ranks buys morale not reach, and the win/loss turns on keeping the line whole and protecting the wings (cavalry's job).
